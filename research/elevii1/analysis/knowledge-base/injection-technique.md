# Subcutaneous Injection Technique

## Overview
Subcutaneous injection technique, proper reconstitution, and correct storage are foundational to a successful peptide protocol. This page covers the creator's exhaustive guidelines on where to pin (stomach, thigh, upper glute), how to completely avoid stinging and injection site reactions, the proper way to mix raw powders with bacteriostatic water, and strict storage rules to prevent expensive compounds from degrading or shattering.

## What The Creator Says
The creator emphasizes that taking peptides is useless if your administration and storage habits are actively destroying the compound or blocking absorption. He consistently warns that "more is not better" and that proper handling—from avoiding the fridge door to swirling instead of shaking—is what actually dictates long-term success. He frames his guides as "idiot-proof" tutorials designed to take the intimidation out of medical prep, while strictly advocating for site rotation to prevent long-term skin damage.

## Uses / Benefits Mentioned
*   **Maximized Absorption:** Rotating injection sites prevents scar tissue buildup, ensuring the peptide actually makes it into the bloodstream. 
*   **Pain Prevention:** Specific techniques (like letting the alcohol dry, diluting the dose, or avoiding the stomach) completely eliminate the severe stinging and burning associated with harsh compounds.
*   **Preserved Potency:** Proper mixing (trickling and swirling) and storage (middle fridge shelf) prevent compounds from turning to jelly, degrading, or losing their metabolic signaling power. 
*   **Cost Savings:** Following the 28-30 day expiration rule for bacteriostatic water and keeping vials off the fridge door prevents users from wasting money on spoiled or shattered peptides.

**The Injection Process**
*   Wipe the top of the BAC water and the peptide vial with an alcohol prep wipe.
*   **Crucial Step:** Wait for the alcohol wipe prep to dry completely on your skin before pinning. **Reconstitution (Mixing) Technique**
*   Extract BAC water using a syringe. Poke the syringe into the raw powder vial at a slight angle so the water trickles gently down the side of the glass. 
*   **Do Not Shake:** Roll the vial gently in your hands or gently swirl it to dissolve the powder. ## Side Effects, Problems, Or Mistakes Mentioned

*   **Lipohypertrophy:** Injecting in the exact same spot repeatedly causes a buildup of hard scar tissue under the skin. This blocks the peptide from properly absorbing into the bloodstream, rendering the dose useless. `[7625363226913475854 | 16500 | 501 | 11 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7625363226913475854]`
*   **Severe Stinging / Injection Site Pain (The "GHK-Cu" Problem):** Highly concentrated peptides (like GHK-Cu) are notoriously harsh and reactive in the skin. Injecting them into sensitive areas like the stomach causes severe burning and stinging that lingers all day. 
    *   *Hack 1 (Location):* Stop pinning the stomach. Move the injection to the upper glute, which has thicker, less sensitive fat. This can completely eliminate the burning sensation. `[7623574383633763598 | 24600 | 620 | 40 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7623574383633763598]`, `[7640208992941231373 | 25900 | 783 | 55 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7640208992941231373]`
    *   *Hack 2 (Dilution):* Draw up your peptide dose into the syringe, then draw an extra 10 to 15 units of plain BAC water into the exact same syringe. This dilutes the concentration right before it hits the tissue. `[7601664372942834974 | 55300 | 1809 | 105 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7601664372942834974]`, `[7640208992941231373 | 25900 | 783 | 55 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7640208992941231373]`
    *   *Hack 3 (Temperature):* Do not inject cold liquid. Let the mixed vial sit at room temperature for 30-45 minutes before injecting to make it a smoother experience. `[7640208992941231373 | 25900 | 783 | 55 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7640208992941231373]`
*   **The "Blue Skin Dot":** Peptides containing copper (GHK-Cu) will leave a distinct blue tint/dot on the skin if pinned too shallowly. It is entirely harmless, is not a bruise, and will fade naturally as the body absorbs the un-dispersed copper. `[7603573661466987789 | 43100 | 1587 | 49 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7603573661466987789]`
*   **Welts and Redness:** Common in people with highly reactive skin during the first few weeks. Raised welts or redness lasting 10-15 minutes post-injection is a normal response as the body adapts. `[7606873183748164894 | 62900 | 2223 | 109 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7606873183748164894]`
*   **Gelling / "Jelly" Peptides:** If a mixed vial (specifically Tesamorelin) turns to jelly, it was likely stored too cold or mixed with cold BAC water. *Fix:* Take it out of the fridge, let it sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes, and swirl it gently in your palm. Do not shake it. If it remains cloudy or jelly-like at room temperature, it has degraded, lost potency, and should be discarded. `[7608743706736200973 | 133500 | 5646 | 104 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7608743706736200973]`, `[7610970723892366606 | 90300 | 3894 | 92 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7610970723892366606]`
*   **Bad BAC Water Indicators:** You know BAC water has expired if there is any visible discoloration, cloudiness, or floating particles ("floaters") in the liquid.
