# Drinking Alcohol on Peptides

## Overview
Drinking alcohol while actively taking peptides—especially GLP-1/GIP agonists like Retatrutide (Reta) and cosmetic peptides like GHK-Cu—requires a major shift in lifestyle habits. Because metabolic peptides fundamentally alter digestion and brain reward pathways, alcohol is processed much slower, leading to delayed intoxication, severe next-day hangovers, and a high risk of accidental over-drinking. Simultaneously, many users experience an unexpected loss of desire to consume alcohol altogether. For cosmetic peptides, alcohol is heavily contraindicated as it actively sabotages skin repair and collagen production.
