# Tirzepatide (Tirz)

## Overview
Tirzepatide (often referred to as Tirz) is a powerful metabolic tool primarily utilized as a single-pathway appetite suppressant. It is highly effective at cutting down "food noise" and assisting users in maintaining a strict calorie deficit. It is frequently compared to, transitioned from, or stacked with Retatrutide (Reta), serving as the standout option for users whose primary struggle is managing pure hunger.

## What The Creator Says
The creator positions Tirzepatide as a stronger baseline appetite suppressant compared to Retatrutide. While Reta focuses on pushing the metabolism harder via multiple pathways (including glucagon activity), Tirzepatide hits hunger, cravings, and food noise noticeably harder. However, the creator firmly warns that weight-loss medications like Tirzepatide are not intended to shut off your appetite completely; if it becomes physically impossible to eat food, your dose is too high. Furthermore, when transitioning off Tirzepatide to newer peptides like Retatrutide, users are cautioned not to chase the instant "Tirz feeling" of appetite suppression, as different compounds build up and act on the body differently.

## Side Effects, Problems, Or Mistakes Mentioned
*   **The "Zero Appetite" Red Flag:** It is a major mistake to increase your dose until you are repulsed by food. If it is impossible to eat on Tirzepatide, the dose is too high and needs to be lowered `[7624614211385101582 | 297900 | 12900 | 272 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7624614211385101582]`.
*   **Hunger & Weight Spikes When Switching:** When users switch from Tirzepatide to Retatrutide, it is completely normal to feel hungrier and even see the scale spike temporarily. Tirzepatide suppresses appetite immediately. Retatrutide takes longer to build up in the system and works on metabolism and insulin sensitivity first before strong appetite suppression kicks in. Low starting doses of Reta will not suppress appetite like a maintenance dose of Tirz does, leading to accidental overeating.
