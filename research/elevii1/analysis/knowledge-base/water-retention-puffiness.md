# Water Retention & Puffiness

## Overview
Water retention and puffiness are common, expected physical reactions when starting a Tesamorelin cycle. As the peptide begins signaling the body to produce and release natural growth hormone (HGH), increased HGH levels pull intracellular water directly into the muscle tissue. While this can cause temporary visual softness or scale fluctuations, it is a well-documented adjustment phase, not an increase in body fat. However, if water retention becomes severe or chronic, it serves as a primary indicator of dosage or protocol mistakes.

## What The Creator Says
The creator categorizes water retention into two distinct phases: normal adjustment and red-flag symptoms. 

During the first 1 to 2 weeks of taking Tesamorelin, users should expect the scale to potentially go up and notice their face or midsection looking slightly softer and puffier. The creator stresses that this is strictly intracellular water within the muscle tissue, not fat gain. This initial puffiness is completely normal and naturally disappears within 1 to 2 weeks as the body adapts to the peptide. 

However, the creator explicitly warns that if water retention and puffiness remain constant beyond this initial adjustment phase, it is a major "red flag" indicating that the dosage is too high or the cycle has gone on for too long.

## Uses / Benefits Mentioned
*   **Muscle Fullness:** While puffiness is generally viewed as an annoyance, the intracellular water retention caused by increased IGF-1 and HGH actually contributes to muscles appearing fuller during the cycle.
*   **Physique Alteration Upon Stopping:** When users stop taking Tesamorelin, the decrease in IGF-1 levels leads to a loss of this muscle water retention, which actively alters the user's physical appearance and physique post-cycle.
