# GHK-Cu

## Overview
GHK-Cu is a highly reactive, naturally occurring copper peptide primarily used for anti-aging, tissue regeneration, and cosmetic improvements. It works by boosting copper activity in the body to stimulate collagen production, increase blood flow, and accelerate skin cell turnover. Because it has a very short half-life and builds up in the system over time, it requires specific, consistent daily dosing rather than a cycled schedule.

## Side Effects, Problems, Or Mistakes Mentioned

**Injection Pain (Stinging / Burning / Welts)**
GHK-Cu is a highly concentrated, reactive solution, making it notorious for causing injection site pain, welts, and redness lasting 10-15 minutes. The stomach is particularly sensitive and can cause burning that lasts all day. 
*   **Hacks to prevent stinging:** 
    1. Inject into thicker fat like the upper glutes rather than the abdomen `[7623574383633763598 | 24600 | 620 | 40 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7623574383633763598]`.
    2. Dilute the dose by drawing an extra 10-15 units of plain BAC water into the syringe before injecting `[7601664372942834974 | 55300 | 1809 | 105 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7601664372942834974]`.
    3. Take the vial out of the fridge and let it sit at room temperature for 30-45 minutes before injecting; injecting cold GHK-Cu increases discomfort `[7640208992941231373 | 25900 | 783 | 55 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7640208992941231373]`.

**"Copper Uglies" (Acne / Breakouts)**
It is completely normal to experience breakouts (the "copper uglies") during the first 1-2 weeks. GHK-Cu accelerates skin cell turnover and repair, pushing underlying inflammation and impurities to the surface. The skin often looks worse before it looks better. Users must remain consistent to push past this phase `[7603155182028918047 | 65200 | 3684 | 83 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7603155182028918047]`.

**Blue Skin Dot**
Pinning too shallowly can leave a blue tint or dot on the skin because the peptide contains copper. This is harmless, is not a bruise, and will fade naturally as the copper disperses and absorbs `[7603573661466987789 | 43100 | 1587 | 49 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7603573661466987789]`.

**Rosacea**
Starting at a dose that is too high or taking it too frequently can overstimulate the skin and trigger rosacea. The fix is to drop the dose down to 1mg and temporarily lower the frequency to every other day `[7609415819235249439 | 51800 | 2319 | 58 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7609415819235249439]`.
