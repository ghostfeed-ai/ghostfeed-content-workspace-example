# Peptide Storage & Shelf Life

## Overview
Proper storage and handling are critical for maintaining the potency and safety of peptides. The creator provides strict timelines and environmental rules for three different phases of peptide use: raw unmixed powder (which lasts 1 to 2 years), reconstituted mixed vials (which expire after 90 days), and punctured bacteriostatic water (which expires in 28 to 30 days). A significant focus of this content is dedicated to avoiding environmental hazards like fridge door temperature fluctuations and troubleshooting storage crises like Tesamorelin turning into "jelly."

## What The Creator Says
The creator stresses that peptides are fragile and expensive tools. Treating storage as an afterthought leads to degraded compounds, lost potency, and wasted money. He heavily emphasizes that mixing a peptide starts a strict 90-day countdown timer, and warns users to plan their vial purchases and cycles around this shelf life so they aren't throwing away expired product. He also urges users to never compromise on the freshness of bacteriostatic water, noting that buying a fresh vial is always cheaper than dealing with the consequences of using spoiled water.

## Uses / Benefits Mentioned
*   **Preserves Potency:** Strict temperature and light control ensures the peptides remain effective for their full 90-day mixed lifespan.
*   **Saves Money:** Buying appropriate vial sizes based on the 90-day expiration window prevents users from having to throw away half-full, expired vials. 
*   **Prevents Degradation & Gelling:** Proper fridge placement and temperature management prevents fragile peptides (like Tesamorelin) from turning cloudy or gelling up.
*   **Ensures Safety:** Adhering to the 28-to-30-day expiration rule for punctured bacteriostatic water prevents the injection of bacteria or degraded liquid.

## Side Effects, Problems, Or Mistakes Mentioned
*   **The Fridge Door Mistake:** Do *not* store mixed vials on the refrigerator door handle or inside the door compartments. The constant opening and closing of the door subjects the fragile peptides to rapid temperature fluctuations and aggressive shaking, which degrades the compound. Furthermore, they are highly prone to falling out and shattering. Always store vials on a stable, middle/interior shelf of the fridge. `[7604270757404773663 | 23400 | 605 | 12 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7604270757404773663]`, `[7641409821882780941 | 163500 | 7902 | 117 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7641409821882780941]`, `[7646541420915952910 | 15100 | 464 | 49 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7646541420915952910]`
*   **Tesamorelin Turning to "Jelly":** Tesamorelin is notoriously fragile. If stored too cold in the fridge, or if mixed incorrectly, it will turn into a cloudy, jelly-like substance. 
    *   *The Fix:* Take the vial out of the fridge, let it sit at room temperature for 10-15 minutes, and gently swirl it in the palms of your hands. **Do not shake it.**
    *   *Prevention:* To avoid gelling during reconstitution, use 2mL of BAC water instead of 1mL to give the powder more room to dissolve. Never use cold BAC water straight from the fridge to mix it; let the water reach room temp first.
    *   *Degradation Warning:* If the Tesamorelin remains cloudy or jelly-like *after* sitting at room temperature, the peptide has degraded, lost its potency, and is no longer viable. `[7608743706736200973 | 133500 | 5646 | 104 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7608743706736200973]`, `[7610970723892366606 | 90300 | 3894 | 92 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7610970723892366606]`, `[7641313103233191182 | 40300 | 1255 | 60 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7641313103233191182]`
*   **Using Expired/Spoiled BAC Water:** Using BAC water past its 28-30 day open window is a major hazard. Visual indicators of spoiled water include discoloration, cloudiness, or floating particles ("floaters").
