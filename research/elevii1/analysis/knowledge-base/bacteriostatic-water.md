# Bacteriostatic Water (BAC Water)

## Overview
Bacteriostatic Water (BAC water) is the essential medical diluent required to reconstitute lyophilized (freeze-dried) raw peptide powders into an injectable liquid form. While raw peptide powders can remain stable for 1 to 2 years if kept in a cool, dark environment, introducing BAC water begins a strict expiration countdown. Handling BAC water correctly—including proper temperature control, swirling techniques, recognizing visual spoilage, and adhering to strict math ratios—is foundational to safe and effective peptide use.

## What The Creator Says
The creator treats BAC water reconstitution as a process that must be "idiot-proofed," frequently referring to his math tutorials as guides "for dummies." He stresses that a "more is better" mindset does not apply to reconstitution ratios. He also emphasizes safety over frugality, stating that buying a fresh vial of BAC water is always cheaper than dealing with the medical problems that arise from injecting spoiled or contaminated liquid.

## Uses / Benefits Mentioned
*   **Reconstitution:** The primary use is dissolving stable powder into a liquid solution that can be drawn into a syringe and injected.
*   **Dilution for Comfort (Stinging Hack):** Highly concentrated peptides (like GHK-Cu) are notoriously reactive and cause stinging or burning upon injection. A major benefit of BAC water is using it as an extra buffer; drawing an additional 10 to 15 units of plain BAC water into the syringe prior to injection dilutes the dose further and mitigates skin irritation and burning. `[7601664372942834974 | 55300 | 1809 | 105 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7601664372942834974]`, `[7640208992941231373 | 25900 | 783 | 55 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7640208992941231373]`

## Routines, Timing, Dosage, Or Process Details

**The Mixing Process:**
1.  **Prep:** Wipe the top of the BAC water vial and the peptide vial with an alcohol prep wipe.
2.  **Extraction:** Extract the required amount of BAC water using a standard 1mL syringe.
3.  **Angle the Needle:** Poke the syringe into the peptide vial at an angle so the water trickles gently down the side of the glass. Do not blast the water directly into the powder.
4.  **Swirl, Don't Shake:** Roll the vial gently in your hands or swirl it to dissolve the powder. Shaking it aggressively damages the peptide and causes gelling. `[7604274823639911711 | 277900 | 14100 | 270 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7604274823639911711]`, `[7641313103233191182 | 40300 | 1255 | 60 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7641313103233191182]`

## Side Effects, Problems, Or Mistakes Mentioned

*   **The 28-30 Day Expiration Rule:** Unopened/unpunctured BAC water is good up to the printed expiration date. However, the moment the cap is popped and a needle punctures the vial, a strict 28 to 30-day window begins. After 30 days, the BAC water goes bad and must be thrown away. `[7632767883646340366 | 167900 | 5149 | 107 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7632767883646340366]`, `[7641726152113016078 | 13300 | 286 | 15 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7641726152113016078]`, `[7649187077866704142 | 23500 | 578 | 45 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7649187077866704142]`
*   **The 90-Day Mixed Peptide Lifespan:** While the BAC water *vial itself* expires in 30 days once opened, once it is mixed into a peptide vial, that specific mixed peptide solution remains potent for approximately 90 days if kept refrigerated. `[7604270757404773663 | 23400 | 605 | 12 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7604270757404773663]`, `[7616527396430236941 | 45600 | 2149 | 78 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7616527396430236941]`
*   **Visual Spoilage Signs:** Safe BAC water must be completely clear. If you observe discoloration, cloudiness, or any floating particles ("floaters") inside the vial, it has spoiled and must be discarded immediately. `[7632767883646340366 | 167900 | 5149 | 107 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7632767883646340366]`, `[7649187077866704142 | 23500 | 578 | 45 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7649187077866704142]`
*   **Storage Mistakes (The Fridge Door):** A massive mistake is storing mixed vials on the refrigerator door handle/shelf. The constant opening and closing causes severe temperature fluctuations and physical shaking, which can degrade the peptide or cause the glass to drop and shatter. Always store vials on a stable, middle inside shelf. `[7607631543531605262 | 70900 | 3104 | 81 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7607631543531605262]`, `[7641409821882780941 | 163500 | 7902 | 117 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7641409821882780941]`, `[7646541420915952910 | 15100 | 464 | 49 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7646541420915952910]`
*   **Gelling / "Jelly" Peptides (Specifically Tesamorelin):** Tesamorelin is highly prone to turning into a cloudy jelly. This is caused by mixing technique errors, not a bad product. 
    *   *Hack to prevent gelling:* Do not use cold BAC water straight from the fridge; let it reach room temperature first. Do not shake the vial. For Tesa, using 2mL of BAC water instead of 1mL gives the powder more dissolving room to prevent gelling. `[7608743706736200973 | 133500 | 5646 | 104 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7608743706736200973]`, `[7641313103233191182 | 40300 | 1255 | 60 | https://www.tiktok.com/@elevii1/video/7641313103233191182]`
