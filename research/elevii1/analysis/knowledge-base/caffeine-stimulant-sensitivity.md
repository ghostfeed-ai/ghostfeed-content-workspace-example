# Caffeine & Stimulant Sensitivity

## Overview
Retatrutide (Reta) fundamentally alters the body's baseline energy and digestion. Because Reta acts on multiple pathways—including glucagon—it naturally boosts resting energy expenditure, stimulates the central nervous system (CNS), and frequently causes an elevated resting heart rate. 

When users combine Reta with external stimulants such as coffee, energy drinks, high-stimulant pre-workouts, fat burners, or nicotine, the stimulating effects are drastically compounded. Furthermore, because Reta slows down gastric emptying, ingested stimulants sit in the digestive tract longer, causing their effects to hit stronger, last much longer into the night, and trigger severe overstimulation. 

## What The Creator Says
The creator warns that combining Reta with heavy stimulants is a common beginner mistake that leads to feeling "awful overall." While consuming caffeine on Reta is not inherently dangerous for everyone, it requires strict monitoring because the user's previous stimulant tolerance is no longer applicable. Stacking strong stimulants on top of a drug that already taxes the CNS frequently results in users feeling wired, anxious, or uncomfortably jittery. 

Additionally, because caffeine and nicotine act as secondary appetite suppressants and diuretics, combining them with Reta can make it dangerously difficult to eat enough daily calories and stay hydrated, ultimately leading to muscle loss and fatigue.
