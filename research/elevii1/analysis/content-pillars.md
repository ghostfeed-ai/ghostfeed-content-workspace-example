## Pillar 1: Educational Protocols & Reconstitution Math (The "For Dummies" Guide)

**What the pillar is about:**
This pillar focuses entirely on the logistics, mathematics, and operational procedures of using peptides. It translates complicated milligram-to-milliliter dosing conversions into simple syringe units and establishes baseline "how-to" guides for beginners. 

**Subtopics:**
*   **Vial Math & Reconstitution:** The standard "golden rule" of adding 1mL (100 units) of bacteriostatic (BAC) water to every 10mg of raw peptide powder to keep unit math universal.
*   **Dosing Schedules & Titration:** Starting at minimum effective doses (e.g., 0.5mg of Reta weekly) and warnings against arbitrary weekly increases. 
*   **Storage & Shelf Life:** Rules for preserving peptides (e.g., mixed vials last 90 days, punctured BAC water lasts 28-30 days). Warnings against storing vials in the fridge door handle due to shaking and temperature fluctuations.
*   **Injection Techniques:** Rotating injection sites to avoid lipohypertrophy, and choosing fatty sites (like glutes) for painful injections like GHK-Cu.
